# Fort Maximillien

## A Stealth Prison-Break in Occupied Waterdeep

*For a wounded 5th-level party. Two PCs free. Two captured. One interrogated upstairs. One awaiting the gallows below.*

> "Peace is maintained, not requested."
> — Colonel Angus, Urban Detachment of the Purple Dragons

## Adventure Summary

**Fort Maximillien** is a fortified garrison and guard post in Waterdeep, held by a **Cormyrean Purple Dragon urban detachment** under the command of **General Peterbilt Varkus**, Marshall of the Western Vanguard and Imperial Governor of Waterdeep.

The fort was built on the ruins of the tavern where the final battle against **Asmodeus** took place at the close of the **Infanta Crisis** — five centuries of holy war that ended with the founding of the Cormyrean Empire. The site was consecrated, then fortified, then forgotten by everyone except the soldiers garrisoned inside it.

Your party is battered at the end of an adventuring day. Two allies are imprisoned inside.

**Tonight's objective (this session):**
- **Extract Lenore** from the locked Infirmary (14) and secure a safe **Long Rest Chamber**.
- **Extract BattleBeast** from the Pens (5).
- Escape alive.

**Next session hooks (do not fully resolve tonight):**
- **Maverick** is held upstairs for interrogation by **General Varkus**.
- **Shadowhigh** is in the dungeon below, scheduled for hanging at dawn unless events change.

## Background & Political Fault Lines

Waterdeep is not a frontier town, and it hates being treated like one.

### The Empire

The Cormyrean Empire was forged five hundred years ago by **Zanzibar the Elder**, the First Emperor of Faerûn, following the defeat of Asmodeus and the end of the Infanta Crisis. The state patron deity **Zarus** — god of sentinels, conquest, and humanity triumphant — blessed the Zanzibar's bloodline to rule forever. Every emperor since has claimed descent from holy blood.

The current emperor, **Zanzibar William the Melancholy**, formerly Prince William Wenly Zarusson, has occupied the Glass Throne for only a month following the death of his elderly father **Zanzibar the Penitent**. Known as a holy and pious man to all but those who knew him, the late emperor gave over nearly all political and military control of the Empire to his armies, his rivals, and his laconic son. The result is an empire loosening at every joint.

### The Factions

**General Varkus (Imperial Governor).** Born in Sembia as the third son of a minor lord, Peterbilt Varkus joined the occupying Cormyrean forces as a squire to Sir Horis Jannassar, a Purple Dragon Knight with roots in Waterdeep and Calamshan. He rose through the ranks of the Imperial Vanguard to command **The Violets**, an elite cavalry and mage detachment known for violet-plumed helms and brutal precision. Their invasion of the Anauroch in DR 1993 was immortalized in Volothamp Geddarm's popular novel *Violets on the Dune*, making Varkus a folk hero.

Zanzibar the Penitent named him Marshall of the Western Vanguard and charged him with the governorship of Waterdeep — as much to guard the Empire's westerly flank as to remove Varkus from the capital.

Now, with the Empire weakening under William the Melancholy, Varkus has proclaimed the **March of the Free West** — declaring loyalty not to the throne, but to the people of the Empire and its most exalted institutions. He is courting Waterdeep's old-money elite and ancient trades — **a new development**. Before he can march on the capital, he must secure his rear, where conspirators in league with the Zanzibar and rival rebellions seek to ruin him.

**The Unmasked Lord ap Dyksterhaus.** Openly flaunts imperial customs, rails against overtaxation, and champions merchant interests. His base is the merchant class and those who profit from them. He refused to visit the imperial capital and pay tribute to the lame duck emperor.

**The Old Houses.** Ancient wealth and older magic. They despise the merchant class but love imperial peace, trade routes, and access. Varkus courting them is a tectonic shift — the old families that predate the Empire now dining with the man who commands its armies.

**The Farmlands.** Revolts flare beyond the city over imperial seizure of grain and cattle. Farmers burn levy wagons. The garrison eats while the countryside starves.

### The Four Rebellions

Varkus is not the only one who smells the Empire's weakness. Across Faerûn, four factions have risen against the Melancholy Emperor:

1. **The Zarus Cult** — A zealot princeling named **Zellmo**, raised by the Horsecock Prophecy to believe himself the new Infanta of Zarus, demands the throne.
2. **The Witch Queen of Myth Drannor** — Princess **Zythara**, once the Emperor's favored cousin and a prodigy of the Arcaneum, was stripped of titles for defying ecclesiastical oversight of arcane study. She fled to Myth Drannor, rekindled its mythal, and now her agents move unseen across the plane.
3. **General Varkus and the Purple Dragons** — The March of the Free West. Loyalty to the Empire's institutions, not its crown.
4. **The Negende Zonen (Zooners)** — Brigands and outlaws in the Anauroch led by **Modle Negen**, who claims descent from the Zanzibars of old.

### Recent Incidents (Tonight)

- A **foiled heist at Tiffany House**, where Varkus was raising funds and making friends among the elite. The party was involved in foiling it.
- A violent incident at **Ard Farkle's**: a convict now held in the fort fired into bystanders. **Carts burned and horses died.** The party was involved.
- Ostara approaches. The festival season is thinner this year. The city's smiles feel rented.

## Running the Fort

This module is built to avoid "one bad roll = TPK." It is a pressure system.

### Zone Alerts (Localized)

Track alert separately for each zone:

| Zone | Area | Rooms |
|------|------|-------|
| **A** | Exterior / Gate | 1–2 |
| **B** | Courtyard & Arcade | 6–9 |
| **C** | Service & Command | 10–13 |
| **D** | Lower Halls | 14–22 |

Each zone has **Alert 0–3**:

| Alert | State | Effect |
|-------|-------|--------|
| **0** | Normal | Routine patrols. |
| **1** | Suspicious | Doubled checks, a runner dispatched. |
| **2** | Active Search | Doors watched, patrols tighten. |
| **3** | Lockdown | Doors barred, reinforcements stage. |

**Fort-wide Lockdown** only happens if the **Dispatch Horn** in (11) is sounded.

### Keys (Two Rings)

**Ring A (on wall pegboard in 11):** Infirmary (14), Back Hall (16), Dungeon stairs (20).
**Ring B (on Colonel Angus):** Stairs Up (12), Alarm Cabinet, upstairs protocols.

**Key Visibility:** The pegboard is visible from the doorway of (11). Ring B is clipped to Angus' sword belt.

### Stairs Up Are Inaccessible Tonight

The **stairs up (12)** are:
- Locked (Ring B)
- Under **Arcane Lock** from above (War Wizard protocol)
- **Barred from the top** (physical bar)

Even with Ring B, opening (12) requires upstairs access. This keeps tonight focused.

### 1. Gatehouse

**Read-Aloud.** The gatehouse smells of wet iron and horse sweat drifting from the pens to the west. Iron-ribbed timber doors, swollen with damp, are set into stone that has seen centuries of weather and war. Two lanterns burn low on hooks, casting pools of copper light across the flagstones. Above, murder slits watch the street with the patience of old architecture. A notice has been nailed to the crossbeam where everyone entering has to duck.

**Occupants.** **Corporal Hendricks** sits on a stool near the inner door, cleaning his nails with a belt knife. **Private Tam** leans against the wall near the murder slits, occasionally peering out at the street. They've been on gate duty since sundown and the boredom has settled into their bones.

**Perception.** DC 12: Hendricks checks the street through the murder slits every four or five minutes, turning his back to the inner door for roughly thirty seconds each time. A PC who notices this pattern gains **advantage on Stealth** checks to slip past during the gap.

**Treasure/Clues.** Notice nailed to the crossbeam: *"By order of the Marshall — Execution at dawn. The prisoner Shadowhigh. Attendance mandatory for all off-duty personnel."* Below it, a grain seizure tally listing three farmsteads stripped this tenday, quantities of wheat and barley scratched in a clerk's hand.

**Pockets.** Hendricks carries a dented pewter flask of Cormyrean apple brandy, half-empty, and a folded duty roster showing which guards are assigned where tonight. Tam has a love letter from a Waterdhavian woman named Sera, written in Chondathan, asking when he's coming home and whether the "purple men" are really as bad as everyone says. He also carries 8 cp and a small wooden comb.

**Dialogue.**
- Hendricks: "Governor was at Tiffany House tonight. Old money. Silk tablecloths. Spellbooks older than Cormyr. Not the kind of people who invite soldiers to dinner."
- Tam: "Since when does Varkus care about dinner parties?"
- Hendricks: "Since the Emperor started looking like he won't last the year."
- Tam: "Dyksterhaus shouts about taxes, but the old houses don't shout. They count bloodlines. They remember things."
- Hendricks: "You sound like you admire them."
- Tam: "I sound like I grew up here."
- If alert rises — Hendricks (calm, standing): "Close the gate. Nobody in, nobody out. Tam, get a runner to the courtyard. Walk, don't run."

**Exits.** South to 2. West (locked) to 5.

**DCs.** Gate lock DC 15 (tools). Quiet entry DC 13 (Stealth).

### 2. Parade Grounds

**Read-Aloud.** A rectangle of hard-packed dirt scored with drill lines and boot-scuffs, open to the night sky. The air is colder here without the walls to hold it. Along the inner wall, fresh-cut timber beams have been stacked with carpenter's care — thick uprights, a crossbeam, and a trapdoor on hinges. It takes a moment to understand what you're looking at. Ballista components lean against the far wall in oiled canvas wraps. A chalkboard near the archway to the courtyard has been wiped and rewritten in a sergeant's careful hand.

**Occupants.** None stationed. Patrols from the courtyard cross this ground every ten minutes, boots crunching on gravel, lantern light swinging.

**Perception.** DC 13: A shadow path runs along the base of the inner wall, where the torchlight from the courtyard archway doesn't quite reach. A PC who spots this can move through the parade grounds with **advantage on Stealth** checks by hugging the wall.

**Treasure/Clues.** The chalkboard reads: *"Ostara drill schedule — 0600. All hands. Full kit. Demonstrate readiness for the citizenry."* The gallows timber smells of fresh sawdust. One beam has tomorrow's date chalked on it.

**Pockets.** No guards stationed. A patrol guard who crosses might carry 2d6 cp and a twist of pipe tobacco.

**Dialogue (passing patrol).**
- "Those beams are for Shadowhigh. They'll have the frame up by dawn."
- "Dyksterhaus won't last if Varkus secures the houses. Merchants bark. Nobles whisper. Steel listens."

**Exits.** North to 1. South to 6.

### 5. Stables / Animal Pens (BattleBeast)

**Read-Aloud.** The stink hits first — straw rot, animal musk, the mineral tang of old blood on iron. Heavy wooden pens line both walls, reinforced with iron bands and bolted to the stone floor. Chains hang from rings set into the wall at shoulder height. In the left pen, something enormous breathes in slow, drugged rhythm, its feathers rising and falling in the lanternlight. In the right pen, BattleBeast kneels in irons, wrists chained to the wall, head down. A single lantern swings on a hook between them, making the shadows shift like something alive.

**Occupants.** **Recruit Wills** sits on an upturned bucket near the door, knees together, spear across his lap. He keeps glancing at the owlbear pen. He is nineteen and looks younger. **Sergeant Drask** leans against the far wall with his arms folded, watching BattleBeast with the professional disinterest of a man who has seen a lot of things chained up. The **Owlbear** is young, captured from the High Forest, sedated with something in its water but not fully asleep. Its talons click against the stone when it shifts.

**Perception.** DC 12: Wills watches the owlbear almost exclusively. He barely glances at the door to area 1 or the corridor to area 10. A PC who notices this gains **advantage on Stealth** checks to enter from either direction while Wills is fixated on the beast. DC 14: The owlbear's sedation is wearing off. Its breathing is quickening. In another hour it will be fully awake and much louder.

**Treasure/Clues.** Drask's sergeant's purse hangs from his belt: 20 gp, a set of bone dice, and a small Cormyrean campaign medal stamped with the Violets' crest. A ration chit nailed to the pen post reads: *"Seizure — Farmland. Requisitioned by authority of the Marshall."* An execution order is tacked beside it: *"BattleBeast. Display in the square at second bell. Shadowhigh. Gallows at dawn."*

**Pockets.** Wills carries a carved bone token of Zarus on a leather thong — his good luck charm — and a crumpled letter from his mother asking him to come home. 3 cp. Drask has a small charcoal portrait of a girl, maybe eight years old, folded into an oilskin square inside his breast pocket. He also carries a ring of stable keys (pens only, not the main locks).

**Secrets.** **Secret door to 10** (DC 14 Perception): A section of the back wall is slightly newer stone, set in different mortar. It opens inward with pressure on the left side, revealing the kitchen passage. Loose chain bolt (DC 14 Investigation): One of BattleBeast's wall bolts has worked loose in its socket. A firm tug (DC 12 Athletics) after finding it snaps it free, halving the time to get BB unchained.

**Dialogue.**
- Wills: "He beat an owlbear once, they said. I heard he tore its beak off with his hands."
- Drask: "Don't matter. Dawn takes them both."
- Wills: "Doesn't seem right. Chaining beasts for show."
- Drask (quiet): "Spectacle prevents revolt. That's the theory. I've never seen it work, but that's the theory."
- Wills (whispering, if PCs are hidden): "Sarge, did you hear something?"
- Drask: "I hear that thing breathing. I hear you talking. I hear my career ending one shift at a time. Go check if it bothers you."
- If BattleBeast stirs: Wills stands up fast, knuckles white on the spear. Drask doesn't move. "Sit down, boy. He's not going anywhere."

**Exits.** North to 1 (locked), south to 10, secret to 10.

**DCs.** Pen locks DC 16; Owlbear chain DC 17.

### 6. Courtyard (Inner)

**Read-Aloud.** The courtyard opens like a stone throat. A well stands at the center, its winch rope frayed and darkened with use. Barrels of rainwater and sand are stacked along the east wall. To the south, the corridor to the command post is marked by a heavier door with iron fittings. To the east, columns open onto the arcade walk. The sky is visible above — clear, cold, and full of stars that don't care about anything happening below them. Gallows timber is piled against the west wall, enough for a scaffold and a drop, with carpenter's marks still fresh on the beams. Three guards stand near the well, talking in low voices.

**Occupants.** **Private Oswin** sits on a barrel, flask in hand. **Private Carver** stands with his arms folded, staring at the gallows wood. **Corporal Belk** paces a slow circuit around the well, occasionally checking the arcade entrance and the corridor to 11.

**Perception.** DC 14: All three guards orient toward the well and each other. The corridor to the arcade (7) sits in their peripheral vision but not their focus — Belk's circuit takes him past it, but his back is to it for roughly forty seconds per loop. A PC who times movement to Belk's rhythm gains **advantage on Stealth** to slip into the arcade unseen.

**Treasure/Clues.** A droplet of violet wax clings to the rim of a water bucket — Varkus' personal seal, the same mark as the Violets' crest. The gallows timber has tomorrow's date chalked on the crossbeam in a carpenter's hand: *"Dawn. Test the drop."*

**Pockets.** Oswin carries a flask of Cormyrean rye whiskey (worth 5 gp to the right buyer), 12 cp, and a crumpled broadside advertising an Ostara market that was cancelled. Carver has a pressed wildflower from his family's farm, flattened between two coins, and a whittling knife that hasn't been used for whittling. Belk carries a patrol schedule and a small leather-bound copy of the Purple Dragon regulations, the pages dog-eared and annotated in his own hand.

**Dialogue.**
- Oswin: "Farmers burned another levy wagon last week. City pretends it's weather."
- Carver (quiet, looking at the gallows wood): "My uncle's farm is on that seizure list."
- Oswin: "Then your uncle should've paid."
- Carver: "Paid with what? They took the cattle in spring. Took the grain in autumn. What's left?"
- Belk: "Keep it down, both of you. The Colonel doesn't like noise."
- Oswin: "The Colonel doesn't like breathing, far as I can tell."
- Belk (sharp): "The Colonel keeps this fort standing. You want to see what happens when it doesn't? Look at Ard Farkle's."

**Exits.** North to 2. East to 7. South to 11.

### 7. Arcade Hall

**Read-Aloud.** A covered stone colonnade runs beneath the outer wall, open to the night air on one side and faced with rough-cut stone on the other. Columns march into the distance, thick as old trees, casting sharp shadows in the torchlight. The wind moves through here in gusts that make the flames dance and lean. You can hear the city beyond the wall — distant laughter, a dog barking, the creak of a cart on cobblestones. The graffiti is scratched into the stone between two columns at about waist height, the letters cut deep enough that someone took their time.

**Occupants.** **Private Norn** walks a slow circuit, torch in one hand, spear slung over his shoulder. He moves like a man who has walked this route a thousand times and stopped seeing it three hundred times ago.

**Perception.** DC 12: Norn reaches the far end of the colonnade and pauses at the last column for a full minute, leaning against the stone and looking out at the city. His back is to the corridor for nearly sixty seconds. A PC who notices this rhythm gains **advantage on Stealth** to move through the arcade while Norn is at the far turn.

**Treasure/Clues.** Graffiti scratched deep into the stone between two columns: *"Waterdeep for Waterdeep."* The letters are old — not tonight's work, not last month's. Someone has tried to scrape them out and failed. Beside them, smaller and newer: a scratched sigil of the Open Lord's office.

**Pockets.** Norn carries a small folded sketch of a woman and a young child, labeled "home" in a shaking hand. He also has 6 cp, a stub of charcoal, and a half-written poem on the back of a requisition form. The poem is about the sea.

**Dialogue.**
- Norn (to himself, if he thinks he's alone): "Ostara is meant to be the beginning of things. Planting. New lambs. Fires for the dead winter. Not… this."
- If he spots something suspicious: "Who's there? Show yourself. I don't want trouble tonight. Nobody wants trouble tonight."
- If confronted by the party: "I'm one man with a spear. You're…whatever you are. I'm not dying for a corridor."

**Exits.** West to 6. East to 8.

### 8. Arcade Corridor

**Read-Aloud.** The colonnade narrows here into a proper corridor, the open arches giving way to solid stone. The torchlight is thinner. The walls press closer. Every footstep bounces between the stones and comes back slightly wrong, slightly louder than it should be. The acoustics of this hallway are a thief's nightmare — or they would be, except for a patch near the midpoint where old straw has been laid across the flagstones, remnants of some long-ago supply transfer that no one bothered to sweep.

**Occupants.** None stationed.

**Perception.** DC 11: The patch of old straw, about ten feet long, creates a natural sound deadener. A PC who spots it and steps on it rather than the bare flagstones gains **advantage on Stealth** checks while moving through this corridor. DC 13: Faint voices carry from the dispatch room (9) — if PCs listen here, they can overhear Loche and Venn's conversation before entering.

**Treasure/Clues.** A dropped seal-strip of wax and ribbon lies in a crack between flagstones: *"Tiffany House — donors pending. Confirm attendance: Lady Vellacourt, Lord Marnivon, Dowager Ilvastarr."* These are names the party may recognize from the old houses.

**Pockets.** None — no guards stationed.

**Exits.** West to 7. East to 9.

### 9. Dispatch Antechamber

**Read-Aloud.** A working room, not a ceremonial one. The desk is scarred with ink stains and knife marks. Maps of the Dock Ward cover the east wall, pinned with brass tacks and marked in red grease pencil — warehouses circled, streets highlighted, supply routes traced like veins. Quills stand in a pot of dried ink. A heavy ledger lies open, its pages covered in a clerk's cramped handwriting. Two soldiers in half-plate sit at the desk, one reading by candlelight, the other with his chin on his fist and his eyes half-closed.

**Occupants.** **Sergeant Loche** reads intelligence reports by candlelight, occasionally making notes in the margin with a thin-nibbed quill. He is sharp, professional, and takes his work seriously. **Corporal Venn** is technically on watch but has been fighting sleep for the last hour. His head dips and catches every few minutes.

**Perception.** DC 14: Venn's drowsiness follows a pattern — he nods off for roughly twenty seconds, jerks awake, checks the corridor, then sinks again within a minute. Loche is absorbed in his reading and his candle throws his shadow across his face, limiting his peripheral vision. A PC who times their approach to Venn's sleep dips gains **advantage on Stealth** to pass through. DC 15: The locked door to 14 has a gap at the bottom wide enough to see a faint blue-white glow — the abjuration wards of the infirmary's long rest chamber.

**Treasure/Clues.** The open ledger details merchant tax arrears: several Dock Ward businesses owe substantial sums, with notes like *"Dyksterhaus affiliate — handle with discretion."* A separate sheet, folded into the ledger, reads: *"Tiffany House — donors confirmed. Purpose: goodwill and continuity fund. Governor attending in person."* Warehouse map on the wall: three are circled in red with the notation *"Powder storage — relocate before Ostara."*

**Pockets.** Loche wears a signet ring bearing a minor officer's rank insignia — not valuable, but it would let someone pass as an officer at a glance. He also carries 15 gp and a sealed letter addressed to his wife in Suzail. Venn has a coded note about an expected promotion, a lucky arrowhead worn smooth from handling, and 9 gp.

**Dialogue.**
- Loche (reading aloud, half to himself): "Dyksterhaus wants the city's coin. Fine. Let him have it. Varkus wants the city's spine. You can't buy that."
- Venn (drowsy): "You think the Governor really means to march?"
- Loche: "I think the Governor means to be ready when the question is asked."
- Venn: "And if Roe doesn't abdicate?"
- Loche (turning a page): "Then the question will be asked differently."
- If alerted: Loche stands immediately, hand on sword. "Venn. Up. Now. Seal the corridor. I'll signal Dispatch."

**Exits.** West to 8. South (locked) to 14.

**DCs.** Door to 14 DC 18 (tools) or Ring A.

### 10. Kitchen & Supplies

**Read-Aloud.** The kitchen is hot and close and smells of onion, rendered fat, and simmered bone. An iron pot the size of a wash basin hangs over banked coals, its contents long since thickened into something between stew and penance. Crates are stacked against every wall — some stamped with the Purple Dragon insignia, some with farmland origin marks, one set apart from the others with wax seals and stenciled warnings. A woman with grey-streaked hair and flour on her forearms stands at the cutting board, working through a pile of root vegetables with the grim efficiency of someone who has stopped caring whether anyone thanks her. Two soldiers sit at a side table, one asleep with his head on his arms, the other cleaning his kit with a rag.

**Occupants.** **Cook Marta** is a civilian contractor from the farmlands outside the city. She was hired when the fort was established and has watched it change from garrison to occupation headquarters with the slow horror of someone who can't leave. **Private Greel** is asleep at the table, face in his arms, off-duty and committed to staying that way. **Private Doss** polishes his buckles and buttons with religious precision, a loyalist who believes a well-maintained kit reflects a well-maintained empire.

**Perception.** DC 12: Marta hums while she works — a droning Waterdhavian folk tune — and she bangs the pots and cutting board with enough force to mask moderate movement sounds. DC 13: The crate stamped *"War Wizard — Restraints"* is sealed but not locked. Beneath the refuse, a rotting sack contains broken seal-strips and torn documents — discarded correspondence that was supposed to be burned. DC 13 Investigation to find: a fragment reading *"…farmland leaders request amnesty in exchange for grain delivery. Rejected by the Marshall."*

**Pockets.** Moss carries a hand-drawn map of sewer routes and back alleys in the Dock Ward, marked with symbols for safe houses and smuggling drops. He has 9 cp and a small knife that isn't regulation. He also has a small pouch of pipe tobacco and a flint.

**Dialogue.**

### 23. Inner Storage

**Read-Aloud.** A dry stone room stacked floor to ceiling with military supplies. Powder kegs sit in cradles against the far wall, stamped with imperial ordnance marks and warnings in three languages. Coils of rope hang from iron hooks. Crates of ballista bolts are stacked four high, their iron tips glinting dully in the torchlight. A sealed wooden box near the door is tied with ribbon and stamped with Varkus' violet seal — it doesn't look military. It looks social.

**Occupants.** None. This room is accessed from the infirmary (14) and is used for storage, not habitation.

**Perception.** DC 13: One of the flagstones near the base of the north wall sits slightly higher than its neighbors, its mortar cracked. It shifts when stepped on. DC 15 Investigation to pry it up reveals a hidden compartment.

**Treasure/Clues.** Ballista bolts — forty in crates, ready for mounting. Powder kegs — six, sealed and dry, extremely dangerous if ignited. Sealed box near the door: inside, a handwritten guest list for the next Tiffany House gathering, annotated in Varkus' hand with notes like *"Lady Vellacourt — sympathetic. Lord Marnivon — undecided. Dyksterhaus favors coin. The one who favors all three wins Waterdeep."* Beside it, a small velvet pouch containing a garnet ring worth 25 gp — someone's emergency fund, hidden and forgotten or abandoned.

**Exits.** West to 14.

## Appendix: DM Quick-Start

1. Start at **(2) Parade Grounds** with distant fort sounds.
2. Put Belk's patrol in **(7)** and the courtyard trio in **(6)**.
3. Make **(14) Infirmary** the "win condition" of the night (safe long rest).
4. Make **(11) Angus** the danger: beatable, but likely avoided.
5. Angus moves between 11, 6, and 13 on a ninety-minute circuit — he can be isolated if timed right.
6. **Surgeon Alaire** in 14 doesn't want bloodshed. **Cook Marta** in 10 resents the occupation. **Private Oster** in 15 sympathizes with the farmers. **Private Moss** in 22 knows the sewers and will negotiate. These are social levers.
7. Every guard has a name, a personality, and something in their pockets. Use them.

> **Design Intent:** Shortcuts are hard (high DC). Long routes are safer. Failure escalates locally; only the horn in (11) escalates globally. The fort is not a dungeon of monsters. It is a disciplined garrison. It is a pressure chamber. Every guard is a person. Every person is a choice.
