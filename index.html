Fort Maximillien

A Stealth Prison-Break in Occupied Waterdeep

For a wounded 5th-level party. Two PCs free. Two captured. One interrogated upstairs. One awaiting the gallows below.

“Peace is maintained, not requested.”

Colonel Angus, Urban Detachment of the Purple Dragons

Adventure Summary

Fort Maximillien is a fortified garrison and guard post in Waterdeep, held by a Cormyrean Purple Dragon urban detachment under the command of General Peterbilt Varkus, Marshall of the Western Vanguard and Imperial Governor of Waterdeep.

The fort was built on the ruins of the tavern where the final battle against Asmodeus took place at the close of the Infanta Crisis — five centuries of holy war that ended with the founding of the Cormyrean Empire. The site was consecrated, then fortified, then forgotten by everyone except the soldiers garrisoned inside it.

Background & Political Fault Lines

Waterdeep is not a frontier town, and it hates being treated like one.

The Empire

The Cormyrean Empire was forged five hundred years ago by Zanzibar the Elder, the First Emperor of Faerûn, following the defeat of Asmodeus and the end of the Infanta Crisis. The state patron deity Zarus — god of sentinels, conquest, and humanity triumphant — blessed the Zanzibar’s bloodline to rule forever. Every emperor since has claimed descent from holy blood.

The current emperor, Zanzibar William the Melancholy, formerly Prince William Wenly Zarusson, has occupied the Glass Throne for only a month following the death of his elderly father Zanzibar the Penitent. Known as a holy and pious man to all but those who knew him, the late emperor gave over nearly all political and military control of the Empire to his armies, his rivals, and his laconic son. The result is an empire loosening at every joint.

The Factions

General Varkus (Imperial Governor). Born in Sembia as the third son of a minor lord, Peterbilt Varkus joined the occupying Cormyrean forces as a squire to Sir Horis Jannassar, a Purple Dragon Knight with roots in Waterdeep and Calamshan. He rose through the ranks of the Imperial Vanguard to command The Violets, an elite cavalry and mage detachment known for violet-plumed helms and brutal precision. Their invasion of the Anauroch in DR 1993 was immortalized in Volothamp Geddarm’s popular novel Violets on the Dune, making Varkus a folk hero.

Zanzibar the Penitent named him Marshall of the Western Vanguard and charged him with the governorship of Waterdeep — as much to guard the Empire’s westerly flank as to remove Varkus from the capital.

Now, with the Empire weakening under William the Melancholy, Varkus has proclaimed the March of the Free West — declaring loyalty not to the throne, but to the people of the Empire and its most exalted institutions. He is courting Waterdeep’s old-money elite and ancient trades — a new development. Before he can march on the capital, he must secure his rear, where conspirators in league with the Zanzibar and rival rebellions seek to ruin him.

The Unmasked Lord ap Dyksterhaus. Openly flaunts imperial customs, rails against overtaxation, and champions merchant interests. His base is the merchant class and those who profit from them. He refused to visit the imperial capital and pay tribute to the lame duck emperor.

The Old Houses. Ancient wealth and older magic. They despise the merchant class but love imperial peace, trade routes, and access. Varkus courting them is a tectonic shift — the old families that predate the Empire now dining with the man who commands its armies.

The Farmlands. Revolts flare beyond the city over imperial seizure of grain and cattle. Farmers burn levy wagons. The garrison eats while the countryside starves.

The Four Rebellions

Varkus is not the only one who smells the Empire’s weakness. Across Faerûn, four factions have risen against the Melancholy Emperor:

  1. The Zarus Cult — A zealot princeling named Zellmo, raised by prophecy to believe himself the new Infanta of Zarus, demands the throne.
  2. The Witch Queen of Myth Drannor — Princess Zythara, once the Emperor’s favored cousin, was stripped of titles for defying ecclesiastical oversight of arcane study. She fled to Myth Drannor, rekindled its mythal, and now her agents move unseen across the plane.
  3. General Varkus and the Purple Dragons — The March of the Free West.
  4. The Negende Zonen (Zooners) — Brigands and outlaws in the Anauroch led by Modle Negen, who claims descent from the Zanzibars of old.

The Western Vanguard

The Western Vanguard is Cormyr’s frontier army — half crusaders, half occupying force — renowned for its discipline and merciless efficiency.

The banner bears the Purple Dragon of Cormyr rampant, armed in dexter foreclaw three arrows argent and sinister foreclaw three lightning bolts of the same; in dexter chief a sun in splendour crowned and charged with an escutcheon; in base a river fimbriated azure, the whole upon a field with sea to sinister and mountains proper with forests vert in dexter chief and base.

The urban detachment garrisoned at Fort Maximillien is not the full Vanguard. These are the soldiers stationed inside a city that tolerates them the way a body tolerates a splinter — with inflammation, not acceptance.

The Violets

An elite cavalry and mage detachment personally loyal to General Varkus. Known for violet-plumed helms and brutal precision, their floral name belies their ruthless reputation in battle. Their invasion of the Anauroch to secure a Netherese ruin from Orcish raiders was immortalized in Volothamp Geddarm’s Violets on the Dune.

The Sword Coast Trading Company

Once the largest mercantile network in Faerûn, dismantled by imperial decree after its founder’s heretical dealings with Wainwright St. Cloud. Acquired and refitted by Revan Darkcember, a titled Lord of Cormyr and a Masked Lord of Waterdeep. Most say it survives in the Underdark and between the Planes. Those who know, know that the Company’s main business is not simply in Faerûn, but spread across the many planes and the Astral Sea itself.
